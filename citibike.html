CitiBike Exploratory Patterns & Behaviors in Tableau

An exploratory Tableau analysis of 2.3 million CitiBike trips revealing usage patterns, station demand, and demographic differences across New York City. This project blends data wrangling, visualization design, and urban mobility insights to tell a clear story about how New Yorkers use the bike‑share system.

Project Overview

This project analyzes over 2.3 million CitiBike trips from 2019 to uncover how riders move across the city throughout the year. The goal was to identify patterns in station demand, trip duration, and rider behavior, and to communicate these insights through an interactive Tableau dashboard. The analysis highlights differences between subscribers and casual riders, seasonal trends, and geographic usage patterns across Manhattan, Brooklyn, and beyond.

Key Insights

1. Weekday vs Weekend Behavior

Weekdays show strong commuter patterns with sharp morning and evening peaks, while weekends shift toward leisure‑based midday usage.

2. Station Demand is Highly Uneven

A small cluster of Manhattan stations accounts for a disproportionate share of trips, revealing opportunities for rebalancing and resource planning.

3. Subscriber vs Customer Differences

Subscribers take shorter, more frequent trips consistent with commuting, while casual riders take longer leisure‑oriented rides.

4. Seasonal Trends

Usage spikes in summer months and drops sharply in winter, highlighting strong seasonality in bike‑share demand.

Technical Approach

  • Cleaned and filtered 2.3M+ trip records
  • Created calculated fields for trip duration, time of day, and rider type
  • Built interactive dashboards with filters for station, date, and rider demographics
  • Used heatmaps, bar charts, and geospatial maps to highlight usage patterns

What I Learned

This project strengthened my ability to work with large datasets, design intuitive dashboards, and communicate insights clearly. It also reinforced the importance of understanding user behavior when analyzing transportation systems and designing visualizations that support decision‑making.
