St. Louis Vacancy Collaborative

Overview

The St. Louis Vacancy Collaborative is a coalition of residents, nonprofits, and city partners working to reduce vacancy and strengthen neighborhoods. I joined the team as the mapping portal was nearing completion, contributing to data clarity, usability improvements, and stakeholder communication to ensure the tool was accessible to both technical and non‑technical audiences.

Technical Overview (Observed)

Source System: LegalServer
  • Operational case‑management platform used by legal aid organizations.
  • Vacancy‑related cases pulled via secure API.
Ingestion Layer: Cloud Functions
  • Automated Node.js jobs calling the LegalServer API.
  • Parsed, validated, and normalized incoming case data.
  • geocoding locations
  • filtering for vacancy‑related cases
Serving Layer: Firebase
  • Real‑time NoSQL database storing curated map‑ready data.
  • Built‑in authentication for secure access.
  • stored the cleaned, curated dataset for visualization layer
Visualization Layer: Mapbox
  • Mapbox for geospatial rendering
  • custom tilesets for smooth performance
  • dynamic overlays for neighborhoods and parcels

My Role

  • Participated in the St. Louis Vacancy Collaborative during COVID through periodic Zoom meetings as part of the data analytics working group. (roughly bi‑quarterly).
  • Observed how the Vacancy Explorer tool was being designed, built, and refined.
  • Learned the data flow, technical architecture, and decision‑making process behind the tool.
  • Asked clarifying questions and offered occasional feedback on data clarity, usability, and community needs.
  • Served as an understudy‑style contributor — not writing code, but gaining a clear understanding of how civic data systems are built and maintained.
